Roy "Doc" Halladay

1977 - 2017

  • 1977 - Roy Halladay was born on May 14th in Denver, Colorado.

  • 1995 - Halladay graduated from Arvada West High School and was selected 17th overall by the Toronto Blue Jays.

  • 1998 - On September 20th Halladay made his MLB debut, defeating the Tampa Bay Devil Rays 7-5.

  • 2001 - After a dismal season in 2000 in which he had a 10.64 ERA, Halladay was demoted to class A Dunedin. He was back in the Blue Jays pitching rotation by mid-season after adjusting his pitching style.

  • 2003 - Halladay's 22-7 record and 3.25 ERA earned him a spot on the American League All-Star team and he went on to win the Cy Young Award.

  • 2005 - A promising season in which he posted a 12-4 record and was selected for the All-Star team was cut short when a line drive off the bat of a Texas Rangers player broke his leg.

  • 2009 - On April 6th Halladay made his seventh consecutive opening day start for the Blue Jays; A team record that still stands today.He was one of the five pitchers named in Sports Illustrated's All-Decade Team. On December 15th The Blue Jays traded him to The Philadelphia Phillies.

  • 2010 - Halladay helped the Phillies clinch the National League East title and finish the season with the best record in MLB. He also became the only pitcher in MLB history to throw a no hitter and perfect game in the same season. He won his second Cy Young Award.

  • 2011 - the "Phantastic Phour" made up of Halladay, Cole Hamels, Roy Oswalt and Cliff Lee was arguably the greatest pitching rotation in MLB history. He pitched a 19-6 record with a 2.35 ERA and nine complete games, leading the Phillies to another NL East Championship and best record in MLB.

  • 2013 - On May 8th Halladay announced he would be undergoing surgery on his shoulder. He pitched his last game on September 23rd before announcing his retirement from baseball due to his injury.

  • 2017 - On November 7th Halladay tragically died after crashing his plane in the Gulf of Mexico.
